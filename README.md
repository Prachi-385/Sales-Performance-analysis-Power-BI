# Visuals Used & Their Purpose

1) Bar Charts
   - Used to compare Sales and Profit across Categories, Sub-Categories, and Customer Segments.
   - Helps identify which areas contribute most to revenue and which require attention.

2) Line Charts
    - Used for Yearly and Monthly Sales Trends.
    - These visuals help in understanding seasonality, growth patterns, and identifying peak sales periods.

3) Maps (Filled Map / Shape Map)
    - Used to show Sales and Profit by State/Region.
    - This helps identify geographical strengths, weak-performing states, and regions with potential growth.

4) KPI Cards
    - Displayed total Sales, total Profit, total Orders, and other key metrics.
    - These give a quick overview of the business performance at a glance.

5) Donut / Pie Charts
   - Used for Segment and Category-level distribution.
   - Helps to quickly understand the proportion of each segment/category in total sales.

6) Slicers (Filters)
   - Added for fields like Region, Category, Segment, Ship Mode, and Date Range.
   - Slicers make the dashboard interactive, allowing users to filter and explore data for specific business questions.

# 4. What the Dashboard Highlights

1) Category Performance: Shows which product categories drive sales and which have lower profit margins.

2) Customer Segment Trends: Helps understand which customer segments (Consumer, Corporate, Home Office) contribute the most to revenue.

3) Regional Sales Insights: Identifies top-performing regions and highlights states facing profit losses.

4) Discount Impact Analysis: Visuals show how higher discount levels affect profitability.

5) Time-Based Performance: Reveals monthly sales trends, seasonal demand, and year-end spikes.

# 5. Key Insights

1) Sales & Profit Overview

- Technology is the most profitable category.

- Furniture has high sales but poor profit, signaling pricing or discount strategy issues.

- High discounts consistently reduce profit, especially in Office Supplies and Furniture.

2) Regional Insights

- West and South regions contribute the highest revenue.

- Certain states show negative profit, mainly due to excessive discounting.

3) Customer Segment Analysis

- Corporate segment drives the highest sales.

- Consumer segment has strong volume but lower profit margins.

4) Time-Based Trends

- Peak sales occur in Novemberâ€“December, likely due to year-end promotions.

- Noticeable growth trend across multiple months and categories.
